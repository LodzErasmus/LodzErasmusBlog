Street Art in Lodz, Poland
  • Romain M.
  • Feb. 13


In recent years, Lodz, Poland has become an important place for street art, with artists coming from all over the world to present their work. The city of Lodz is known for its industrial heritage and iconic textile industry, but also in recent years for its street art, which is ubiquitous in the city.

The art scene in Lodz has been developing since the late 1980s, when graffiti started to appear all over the city. After that, more and more artists from different countries and backgrounds came to the city to express their unique styles and perspectives. The city has also hosted a number of festivals and events focused on street art, such as the Street Art Festival, which celebrates the work of local and international artists. Lodz's street art is incredibly diverse, ranging from small graffiti pieces to large-scale murals. There are also many different styles, from traditional graffiti to abstract and surrealist works. Many works are inspired by the city's history, such as the mural The Polish Manifesto, which pays tribute to the textile industry, and the mural The Jewish Slum, which pays tribute to the Jews living in Lodz during World War II. Lodz street art also includes more fun and interesting pieces, such as the "Cat Wall," which features dozens of cats painted on the city's walls.

INTI “Holy Warrior”

What makes Lodz street art special is that it showcases the city's unique culture and history. The works reflect the struggles and victories that Lodz has experienced over the years. Street art offers residents and visitors a glimpse into the city's vibrant culture.



Lodz's art scene is a testament to the city's creativity, diversity and resilience. With more and more artists coming to exhibit their work in the city, Lodz is increasingly known as an international center of street art. From traditional graffiti to large-scale murals, there is something for everyone in the Lodz art scene.

The Light Move Festival brings together no less than 650,000 people over the course of a weekend. Building illuminations, light installations and projections, 3D-mapping, laser shows and more. Everything that is most spectacular in the field, come to dazzle the tourists in a city plunged into darkness.



The Light Move Festival

With the great diversity of artworks and styles, we will be able to visit many alleys decorated with the most beautiful works of international artists during our Erasmus. This will enrich our culture of street art and art in general.
